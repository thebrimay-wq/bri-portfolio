Smart Benefits

Taking a CEO's idea from concept to live enterprise SaaS — a benefits integration platform for Recordkeepers and an AI-powered chat widget giving plan participants instant access to their benefits.

01
The Challenge

Benefits information, buried and inaccessible

For employees, understanding their benefits has always been a friction-filled, multi-tab, call-HR experience. For Recordkeepers managing plan data on the back end, integration was fragmented and manual. There was no unified experience connecting both sides. The CEO had a vision — I was handed a blank canvas and a mandate to make it real.

Enterprise SaaS B2B2C Recordkeeper Integration Benefits Technology
02
The Opportunity

Two audiences, one coherent system

The opportunity was dual: streamline the back-end benefits integration workflow for Recordkeepers, and give plan participants (employees) a front-end chat experience where all their benefits information lives in one intelligent, conversational interface. Solve both, and you change the category.

03
Creative Vision

From idea to product — designed pixel by pixel

I translated the CEO's concept into a full product vision: a dual-sided platform with a clean, trust-forward SaaS dashboard for Recordkeepers and an embedded AI chat widget for plan participants. Every screen, every interaction, every state — I designed it. Then I sat with the dev team and built it, iterating week by week as the product took shape in real clients' hands.

Trustworthy Conversational Enterprise-Grade
04
Design & Build

Hands-on from concept to code

This wasn't a hand-off project. I designed the SaaS platform UX and UI — dashboards, data views, integration flows, admin controls — and the front-end widget that employees interact with. Then I worked directly with the engineering team to implement, QA, and iterate. Every release, every bug, every pivot: I was in it. A year and a half later, I still am.

Collaboration

Designer + developer, side by side

Smart Benefits is the kind of project that only works when design and engineering are in constant dialogue. I was embedded with the dev team throughout — not handing off specs and waiting, but in the code reviews, the standups, the "this doesn't render right" conversations. That proximity is why the product shipped, and why it keeps improving.

Aimee AI

Transforming a stagnant Q&A search engine into a safe, proprietary AI financial coach — giving everyone a knowledgeable guide in their pocket.

01
The Challenge

A search engine pretending to be a coach

Financial Finesse had a question-and-answer search tool that was, functionally, a keyword search — static, impersonal, and increasingly out of step with how people expect to access information. Users wanted answers, not search results. They wanted a conversation, not a lookup table.

AI Product Financial Coaching Conversational UI Proprietary AI
02
The Opportunity

A financial coach for everyone — safely

The opportunity was to build something rare: an AI that felt genuinely helpful and human, but was rigorously safe. Trained exclusively on Financial Finesse's proprietary content and expertise, Aimee couldn't hallucinate outside the guardrails — giving users the warmth of a conversation and the reliability of a trusted source.

03
Creative Vision

Give the AI a name. Give her a voice. Give her a face.

I made the decision to personify the AI — not as a generic chatbot, but as a named, branded character: Aimee. Naming her created trust and approachability. It signaled that this wasn't a generic tool bolted onto the platform — it was a deliberate, designed experience with its own identity, tone, and personality. The design had to make Aimee feel like someone you'd want to talk to about money.

Approachable Trustworthy Safe Knowledgeable
04
Design

Every pixel, designed with purpose

I designed the full Aimee experience: the conversational UI, the response format system, the visual identity, the onboarding flow, and the tone guidelines that made her feel consistent. The interface had to balance warmth with authority — too casual and she'd lose trust, too corporate and she'd lose the human quality that made her worth talking to.

Financial Finesse Hub Redesign

A blank-page redesign of the core product, delivered as a fully functional demo in 2 months — then continuously refined through user testing and iteration.

01
The Challenge

A core product that had lost its edge

The Financial Finesse Hub — the company's flagship product — had grown dated. It worked, but it no longer felt modern or competitive. The CEO didn't want a refresh. She wanted a blank page redesign. And she wanted a fully functional demo in two months.

Core Product UX Redesign High-Pressure Delivery User Testing
02
The Opportunity

A rare chance to start over — with pressure that makes you sharp

When a CEO says "blank page," that's both terrifying and liberating. Every assumption about the product was on the table. The constraints — 2 months, fully functional, CEO reviewing daily — forced clarity. No over-engineering. No endless iteration in Figma. You design what you can ship.

03
Creative Vision

Modern, intuitive, built for the user who's already busy

The Hub needed to feel like it belonged in 2024 — not 2014. The redesign recentered the experience around clarity and action: less clutter, more signal, faster paths to the things that matter most. Financial wellness tools fail when they're overwhelming. We designed for the employee who has 10 minutes, not 10 hours.

Modern Intuitive Action-Oriented
04
Process

Design it. Build it. Pivot. Repeat.

This project was an all-hands-on-deck sprint. I designed the UX and UI from scratch, got it dev-ready, then sat with the engineering team as we coded it in real time. Every day brought new feedback from the CEO — scope changes, direction pivots, new priorities. I held the design vision while staying completely flexible in execution. That combination — conviction plus adaptability — is what got the demo done on time.

After launch, we ran user testing rounds and I implemented fast follows — UX and UI updates based on what real users actually struggled with. That work continues today.

Collaboration

CEO-driven, team-executed, designer-led

This was a project where every decision mattered and every day counted. I worked directly with the CEO, translating her evolving vision into design decisions that the engineering team could act on immediately — without losing velocity. Managing the creative direction while the scope shifted daily was the real design challenge.
