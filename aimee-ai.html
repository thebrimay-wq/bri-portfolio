Case Study — AI Experience Design

Aimee AI

Redesigned a static Q&A tool into a CFP-backed AI coach — now reaching over 1 million employees

Aimee started as a keyword search tool. I helped transform it into a proactive, empathetic coaching experience that gives employees personalized financial guidance — backed entirely by CFP expertise, not the open internet.

Role
Product Design Lead
Company
Financial Finesse
Timeline
2024–2025
Scope
AI UX · Conversation Design · Product Strategy · Benefits Integration

Before & After

Static search → real coaching.

The Problem

Access to content isn't the same as guidance.

Employees had a library of financial resources. What they didn't have was a way to connect that information to their own situation, their benefits, and their next move.

User Problem

Nobody knew where to start

  • Keyword search required users to already know what to ask
  • No connection between content and the employee's actual benefits
  • Sessions ended without a clear action or next step
  • Low engagement and even lower return visits

Business Problem

Generic AI wasn't an option

  • Financial decisions are high-stakes — bad information causes real harm
  • Internet-sourced AI tools carry bias, inaccuracy, and liability risk
  • The product needed to be trustworthy enough for employers to embed in their benefits platforms

The Approach

Design the trust first. Then design the conversation.

Before touching UI, I focused on the strategic foundation — what would make Aimee safe enough to trust with real financial decisions, and human enough to actually coach.

01

Closed, expert-backed system

All financial content comes exclusively from Financial Finesse CFPs. Gemini handles language understanding — but zero financial guidance comes from the open internet.

02

Coach, not chatbot

Modeled on how a real CFP coaches — listen, reflect, ask a clarifying question, then guide. Every exchange ends with a clear next step.

03

Benefits-integrated

Aimee connects guidance to each employee's actual employer benefits — making advice specific, not generic. Embeddable as a widget or API on any intranet or benefits portal.

04

Built to scale

Designed to work inside the Financial Finesse Hub and as a deployable product for employers — including white-label partnerships like SmartBenefits on recordkeeper sites.

Learnings

What I'd tell a designer starting this project.

  • 01

    Responsible AI is a design advantage, not a constraint.

    The closed system wasn't a limitation — it was the product's biggest differentiator. Leaning into it made the design sharper and the trust story clearer.

  • 02

    Coaching has a structure. Design for it.

    The best financial coaches don't just answer questions — they listen, reflect, ask a clarifying question, then guide. Mapping that arc gave the conversation design a backbone that made every interaction feel intentional.

  • 03

    Embeddability changes the design brief entirely.

    A widget on a recordkeeper's site has a different entry point, user expectation, and trust context than the same product inside a full platform. Designing for both required holding two very different user mental models simultaneously.
